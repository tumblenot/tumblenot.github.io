Pre-trip notes

Read before you fly
Apps — download before the trip
  • Meituan (美团) — food delivery, restaurant bookings, reviews
  • Dianping (大众点评) — like the Chinese Yelp, good for checking ratings and reviews
  • Alipay (支付宝) — essential for payments, also works for Meituan ordering
  • WeChat (微信) — need this for everything — payments, restaurant reservations, calling ahead, QR codes

Set all of these up before you land. Some restaurants only take reservations through Dianping or WeChat, and a lot of street stalls and smaller places don't accept cash or card.

Before you pay for any meal or in any store, always check if there's a promo running on Dianping or Meituan first. A lot of places have discounts, vouchers, or set meal deals on these apps that you won't see on the menu.
Alipay and WeChat Pay add a service charge on bills above RMB 200. If you want to avoid it, just split the bill or ask if they take credit card. Most places are quite flexible about it, unless it's a massive amount.
Food — Eat In or Order on Meituan
  • Quick rule of thumb: anything above 4.2 stars is good. Don't chase the 5-star places — ratings that perfect are usually bought 😂 Aim for 4.2–4.7 and you're in safe territory.
  • You can just pop in or order for delivery on Meituan, if you're having a lazy day at the hotel.
Getting around
  • Get a Shanghai Metro transport card at the airport (¥20 deposit, reloadable) — works on all lines and buses
  • Download Baidu Maps (better than Google for Shanghai metro navigation — works without VPN)
