Shopping
Wukang Road Heritage Walk
French Concession • 2–3 hrs • Best: morning (less crowded)
Walk + cafés
  • What to do: Apoli Ita Bakery (start) → Wukang Mansion (photos) → north up Wukang Rd → Anfu Rd: 13 De Marzo café (teddy bear drinks), D.Desirable (Dylan Wang’s store) → Yanqing Rd: Monday Sleeping Club (No.72), Goodbai Café (No.92)
  • Tips: Start by 9am for best photos and no crowds.
Huaihai Middle Road
French Concession → Puxi • ~3 hrs • Best: mid-morning to afternoon
Shopping street
  • What to do: HAI550 (Matcha Wang sheep matcha) → Harbin Food Factory → Cathay Cinema (100-yr-old iconic cinema) → Songmont, Mason Prince, Gentle Monster, sports stores
  • Tips: Eat Harbin butterfly pastries on the spot — goes soft.
TX Huaihai
616 Huaihai Middle Rd • 1–2 hrs • Best: afternoon
Pop-ups
  • What to do: Pop-up stores; young local designer brands; street culture; MUVA (great quality leather bags)
  • Tips: Pair with bubble tea: Heytea / KOI / Cha Bai Dao
Jing’an Kerry Centre + Zhangyuan
Jing’an, Nanjing West Rd • ~2 hrs • Best: afternoon
Luxury + heritage
  • What to do: Jing’an Kerry Centre (luxury mall; AMAM Bakery inside), Zhangyuan (beautifully restored shikumen), luxury concept stores, Adidas restocks, Louis Vuitton “The Louis” ship installation (HKRI)
Xintiandi + Dongtai Li
Huangpu • ~2 hrs • Best: any time
Shikumen
  • What to do: North Block: 1920s stone-gate lane houses (cafés and boutiques inside), Le Labo flagship, Dongtai Li (newer extension; modern luxury lifestyle)
  • Tips: Better morning than afternoon; IG-worthy at night too.
Museums & culture
Rockbund Art Museum / Waitanyuan
Yuanmingyuan Rd, near Bund • 1.5–2 hrs
Art + architecture
  • What to do: 1920s–30s Art Deco buildings; contemporary art exhibitions; nearby: To Summer (观夏), Songmont, THE BEAST café.
  • Tips: Closed on Mondays. Best before 10am. Walk to the end for Pearl Tower view across the water.
Shanghai Science & Technology Museum
2000 Century Ave, Pudong • 3–4 hrs
Family-friendly
  • What to do: World of Robots; Children’s Technoland; IMAX 4D cinema; Earth Exploration; Space Hall.
  • Tips: Book IMAX in advance. Food court inside.
EKA·Tianwu Industrial Art Park
535 Jinqiao Rd, Pudong • 2–3 hrs
Industrial art park
  • What to do: 100-acre free outdoor industrial art park; parent-child interactive zones; street art; Lujiazui skyline backdrop.
  • Tips: Free entry. Combine with Century Park; pedal boats nearby.
Shanghai Museum
People’s Square • 2–3 hrs
Ancient Chinese art
  • What to do: Ancient Chinese art.
  • Tips: Free entry but reservation required.
Yuyuan Garden (豫园) + Old City
Huangpu, near Bund • 1.5–2 hrs
Classic garden
  • What to do: Ming dynasty classical garden (1559); stone bridges, pavilions, koi ponds.
  • Tips: Closed Mondays. Combine with Bund walk.
Le Labo Shanghai Flagship (Capella / Xintiandi)
Xintiandi shikumen building • 30–45 min
Design stop
  • What to do: 1920s shikumen house. Ground floor: fragrance boutique. Upstairs: vegan café.
Spa
Zhimu (指沐)
Jing’an (Wanrong Rd 280) • Private room (4-hr block)
Massage + F&B
  • What to do: Private room (4-hr block); 80–90 min aromatherapy full body massage; full F&B menu included.
  • Tips: Book night before via WeChat/Meituan. Book on Meituan for ~20% discount.
Shuiguo Hot Spring (水裹)
Multiple outlets • Walk-in
Family-friendly
  • What to do: Hot spring pools + massage add-on; (Wujiaochang outlet) bowling + arcade; claw machines; kids’ playground/ball pit; billiards/pool; lounge; free buffet.
  • Tips: Buy Meituan voucher for ~20–30% off.
Shangyin (上引)
Multiple outlets • Book ahead
All-in-one
  • What to do: Movie theatre; PS5; VR games; arcade; karaoke pods; billiards; hot spring pools; beauty/massage section; buffet.
  • Tips: Book ahead.
Peninsula Spa
3/F The Peninsula, 32 Zhongshan E 1st Rd • Book ahead
Luxury
  • What to do: Forbes 5-Star rated; half-day packages (4 hrs); pool/sauna/steam included; adults only.
  • Tips: Book ahead (premium pricing).
Dragonfly (悠庭)
559 Nanchang Rd + multiple French Concession outlets • Walk-in or call ahead
Massage
  • What to do: Stack 90-min sessions.
  • Tips: Walk-in or call ahead.
Outdoors
Gongqing Forest Park
2 Junxiu Rd, Yangpu • 3–5 hrs • Best: morning (arrive by 9am)
Big park
  • What to do: Horse riding; go-karts; zip-line; paintball; rowboats; frisbee golf; largest urban park in Shanghai; autumn foliage; toddler area.
  • Tips: Metro Line 8. Pack snacks. ¥10 park entry. Activities ¥30–100 each.
Century Park + Pedal Boats
Century Ave, Pudong • 2–3 hrs • Best: any time
Pudong
  • What to do: Largest park in Pudong; pedal boats; bikes available; autumn foliage; picnic-friendly.
  • Tips: Free entry. Combine with Science Museum or EKA.
Sheshan Forest Park + Hilltop Basilica
Sheshan • 2–3 hrs • Best: morning
Hike
  • What to do: Short hike up Sheshan Hill (208m) for panoramic views; Gothic-style Basilica at the summit (built 1925); observatory; bamboo groves.
  • Tips: Metro Line 9; ~25 min walk or cable car up.
Zhujiajiao Ancient Water Town + Canal Boat
Zhujiajiao • 3–4 hrs • Best: after Sheshan
Water town
  • What to do: 36 ancient stone bridges; Ming dynasty stone-paved lanes; gondola canal boat; local snacks along the way.
  • Tips: Anh Lu restaurant for lunch. Metro Line 17 or minibus.
Xixi Wetland Park
Hangzhou • 2–3 hrs
Hangzhou
  • What to do: Golden reeds and persimmons; gondola boat rides; Ding Sheng sticky rice cake workshop.
Legoland Discovery Centre
5/F, 211 Century Ave, Pudong (Line 2) • 2–3 hrs • Best: any time
Indoor
  • What to do: LEGO rides; 4D cinema; LEGO workshop; Miniland Shanghai. Fully indoor; air-conditioned; for 5–10 year olds.
  • Tips: Book in advance. Timed entry.
